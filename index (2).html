📜 Ramadan & Medicine: How to Survive Exams & Earn Barakah! 📜

✍️ Written by Academic structures– JUMJ [PC-I]

Bismillah...

Ramadan is here, and so are the EXAMS—that ultimate test of patience, endurance, and last-minute dua strategies! 📚😅as medicine students, we must balance reading, attending classes, standing for Taraweeh, reciting Qur’an, and, of course, surviving those long fasting hours. Let’s talk about how to handle it all like a pro!

📖 1. When & Where to Read?

  • Early Morning (After Suhoor & Fajr): Best time for deep study. Your brain is fresh, your stomach is temporarily full, and shaytaan is *chained*! 📚
  • After Dhuhr: Medium-focus topics. Avoid “hardcore” subjects—your energy is dropping.
  • Before Maghrib: Quick revision, NOT heavy reading. Your mind is on Iftar!

📚 2. Attending Classes: How to Survive Without Coffee ☕🚫

  • Hydrate Well at Suhoor (Water, Dates, Fruits—Not “Just Tea & Sleep”😁).
  • Power Naps: 20 minutes after Dhuhr or Asr can save your neurons from burning out!
  • Dua Before Entering Class: "Rabbi zidni ilma" (Surah Taha: 114).

🕌 3. Taraweeh: The Med Student’s Leg Day!😅 🦵

Don’t skip Taraweeh because of “exam stress.” Your brain needs spiritual fuel too! Stretch before standing (yes, warm-up for Salah is officially recommended😁). Avoid “Sujood sleeping syndrome” by eating light at Iftar!

📖 4. Qur’an Reading & Memorization

Set a goal for at least 1 Juz per day. If you can memorize drug classifications[pharmaco], you can do this too!

✅ 5. Best Exam Approaches & Recommendations

  • Time Blocking: Allocate fixed times for study, worship, and rest.
  • Group Discussions: Revise with friends—helping each other is sadaqah!
  • Dua Before Studying:

    اللهم انفعني بما علمتني وعلمني ما ينفعني وزدني علما

    “O Allah, benefit me from what You have taught me, teach me what will benefit me, and increase me in knowledge.

🤲 Final Reminder: Keep Your Intention Pure!

Remember, our studies in medicine are also an act of worship if done with the right intention—to help others, serve the ummah, and seek Allah’s pleasure. May Allah grant us ease, success, and the ability to balance our deen and dunya!

✨ Ameen ya Rabb! ✨
